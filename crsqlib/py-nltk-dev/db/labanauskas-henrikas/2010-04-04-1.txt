Homage to Chilean politician Jaime Guzm&aacute;n nineteen years after his death
2010-04-04
April 4, 2010, Published, Archived, Chile, South America, Augusto Pinochet, Politics and conflicts, Sebasti&aacute;n Pi&ntilde;era, Santiago, Chile, Santiago Metropolitan Region, 
The Chilean President Sebasti&aacute;n Pi&ntilde;era and other center-right politicians attended last Thursday morning the Memorial to Jaime Guzm&aacute;n to celebrate the 19th anniversary from the death of the founder of the Uni&oacute;n Dem&oacute;crata Independiente (UDI) (Independent Democratic Union).After the ceremony, a pilgrimage to the General Cemetery and a mass, place that in the past the ceremony was made completely in memoriam to the dead senator. In the monument, the President Pi&ntilde;era and Juan Antonio Coloma, UDI president, did some speeches, in which they emphasized the Guzm&aacute;n politic figure and they emphasized it as the first remembrance from his death in a center-right government.Jaime Guzm&aacute;n was a constitutionalist lawyer, collaborator in the dictatorship of Augusto Pinochet, one of the redactor of the Chilean Politic Constitution of 1980, and founder of the UDI. Guzm&aacute;n was killed in April 1, 1991 when he was going out the Western Campus of the Pontificia Universidad Cat&oacute;lica de Chile. He was taken to the Militar Hospital of Santiago, but he died 3 hours later. He was killed by the Frente Patri&oacute;tico Manuel Rodr&iacute;guez (Manuel Rodr&iacute;guez Patriotic Front), and according to recent investigations, he was killed specifically by Ricardo Palma and Ra&uacute;l Escobar, and his assassination was being planned since the eighties.
